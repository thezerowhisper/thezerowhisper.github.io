Pregnancy Wheel — Clinical Reference Guide

What is a Pregnancy Wheel (OB Wheel)?

A pregnancy wheel (OB wheel / obstetric wheel) is a circular slide-rule tool used by healthcare professionals to calculate gestational age and estimated due date (EDD) from the last menstrual period (LMP). It applies Naegele's Rule: EDD = LMP + 280 days, adjusted for cycle length. This online version extends that calculation with 10+ clinical tools for obstetric practice.

Gestational Age Calculators

GA by LMP: Enter your LMP and cycle length to get EDD, gestational age in weeks and days, trimester, and conception estimate. GA by Scan: Back-calculates LMP and EDD from ultrasound dating — first-trimester CRL is more accurate than LMP for irregular cycles. GA on Given Date: Find exact GA on a specific date for operation theatre notes, discharge summaries, or referral letters. Reverse (EDD → LMP): Back-calculate LMP and conception date when only the EDD is known.

Fetal Assessment Tools

EFW — Hadlock 1985: Gold-standard biometric formula for Indian obstetric practice. Accepts 4 parameters (BPD+HC+AC+FL) or 3 when HC is unavailable. Includes SGA/AGA/LGA classification. EFW carries ±15% variability. Apgar Score: Newborn assessment at 1 and 5 minutes — scores Appearance, Pulse, Grimace, Activity, and Respiration.

Obstetric Scoring & Risk Tools

Bishop Score: Cervical favourability for labour induction — score ≥9 allows direct oxytocin; below 6 requires ripening. VBAC/TOLAC: 8-point FOGSI/ACOG eligibility checklist. PPH Risk: 15-factor weighted scoring per RCOG/FOGSI with specific management protocols.

GDM Screening — DIPSI & FOGSI Protocol

DIPSI recommends universal non-fasting 75g OGTT for all pregnant Indian women. Blood glucose ≥140 mg/dL at 2 hours = GDM. FOGSI mandates screening at first ANC visit and at 24–28 weeks; high-risk women get an additional screen at 32–34 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a pregnancy wheel and how does it work?

A pregnancy wheel applies Naegele's Rule to calculate gestational age and due date from the last menstrual period. Set the LMP on the wheel, and the corresponding date shows the EDD. This online pregnancy wheel does the same with additional features like cycle-length adjustment, scan-based dating, and milestone tracking.

How many weeks pregnant am I?

Open the "Pregnancy Wheel — Gestational Age" section above, select "GA by LMP", enter your LMP, and press Calculate. Pregnancy is counted from the first day of your last period — not from conception.

How accurate is an online pregnancy wheel?

An online pregnancy wheel is as accurate as Naegele's Rule — which assumes a 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14. For non-standard cycles, use the cycle-length selector. For the most accurate dating, a first-trimester ultrasound (CRL at 7–12 weeks) is superior to LMP-based dating.

Which Hadlock formula is used for EFW in India?

The Hadlock 1985 formula is standard in Indian obstetric practice. The 4-parameter formula (BPD+HC+AC+FL) is preferred; 3-parameter (BPD+AC+FL) when HC is unavailable. EFW estimates carry ±15% variability.

What Bishop score indicates a favourable cervix?

Bishop score ≥9: highly favourable — direct oxytocin induction appropriate. Score 6–8: favourable. Score below 6: unfavourable — cervical ripening with prostaglandins (PGE2) or Foley catheter recommended.

What is the DIPSI GDM screening protocol?

DIPSI recommends a single-step, non-fasting 75g OGTT. Blood glucose ≥140 mg/dL at 2 hours = GDM. FOGSI recommends universal screening at first ANC visit and 24–28 weeks, with an additional screen at 32–34 weeks for high-risk women.
