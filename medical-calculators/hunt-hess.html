Hunt-Hess Scale & SAH Management

GradeClinical FeaturesMortality
IAsymptomatic or mild headache~5%
IIModerate-severe headache, neck stiffness~10%
IIIDrowsiness, confusion, mild deficit~30%
IVStupor, hemiparesis~50%
VDeep coma, decerebrate~80%

Clinical Guide

  • Early Treatment: Treatment of the aneurysm (coiling or clipping) within 24–48h is usually recommended for grades I–III.
  • Vasospasm: Peak risk occurs between day 4 and 14. Nimodipine is standard of care.
  • Hydrocephalus: Common complication; may require EVD.
⚠ Medical Disclaimer: Hunt-Hess grading is for clinical reference. SAH requires urgent neuroimaging and specialist neurosurgical consultation.
