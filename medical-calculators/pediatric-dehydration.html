WHO Rehydration Plans

  • Plan A (No/Minimal Dehydration — Score 0–1): Home treatment with ORS. Give 10 mL/kg ORS for each loose stool passed. Continue breastfeeding. Zinc 20 mg OD × 10 days (10 mg if <6 months). Return if worsening
  • Plan B (Some Dehydration — Score 2–4): ORS 75 mL/kg over 4 hours in clinic. Reassess every hour. If vomiting: 5–10 mL/kg NG ORS. After rehydration, continue Plan A. Zinc 20 mg OD × 10 days
  • Plan C (Severe Dehydration — Score ≥5): IV Ringer's Lactate (preferred) or NS: 100 mL/kg. Children: 30 mL/kg over 30 min, then 70 mL/kg over 2.5 hours. Reassess every 15–30 min. Start ORS as soon as child can drink. Admit to hospital

Zinc Supplementation — Why It Matters

WHO and UNICEF recommend zinc supplementation for all children with diarrhoea: 20 mg/day for children ≥6 months, 10 mg/day for infants <6 months, for 10–14 days. Zinc reduces the duration of diarrhoea by ~25%, reduces stool output, and reduces the risk of subsequent diarrhoeal episodes for up to 3 months. In India and South Asia, zinc supplementation is part of the national diarrhoea management protocol alongside ORS.

Red Flags — When to Refer Urgently

  • Severe dehydration (Plan C) — always admit
  • Bloody diarrhoea (dysentery) — antibiotic therapy needed
  • High fever (>39°C) with diarrhoea in infants <6 months
  • Persistent vomiting preventing ORS administration
  • Abdominal distension — possible ileus or obstruction
  • Worsening dehydration despite Plan B
