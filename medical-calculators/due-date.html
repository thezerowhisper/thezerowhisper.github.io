Understanding your Due Date (EDD)

Your Estimated Due Date (EDD) is typically 40 weeks (280 days) from the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). In medical practice, this is calculated using Naegele’s Rule. However, only 5% of babies arrive on their exact due date.

Indian Pregnancy Scan Schedule

In India, obstetricians usually follow this standard timeline for scans and tests:

Baby Size: Indian Context

  • Week 8: Size of a Jamun (Embryo becomes a fetus).
  • Week 12: Size of a Nimbu (Lemon).
  • Week 20: Size of a Banana (Halfway mark).
  • Week 28: Size of a Papaya (Viability milestone).
  • Week 40: Size of a Kaddu (Pumpkin).

Pregnancy Diet & Nutrition in India

Nutrition is vital for fetal brain development and maternal health. Indian guidelines recommend:

  • Folic Acid: Mandatory in the first trimester to prevent neural tube defects.
  • Iron & Calcium: Started from the second trimester (after 14 weeks) to prevent maternal anemia and support baby's bone growth.
  • Protein: Include Paneer, Dal, Eggs, and Soya for optimal fetal growth.
  • Avoid: High mercury fish, unpasteurized dairy, and excessive caffeine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I calculate my due date after IVF FET?

Yes. For a Day 5 Blastocyst transfer, the calculation is: Transfer Date + 261 days. IVF due dates are often more accurate because the date of fertilization is known precisely.

What is "Full Term" in India?

A pregnancy is considered full term between 37 and 40 weeks. Most doctors in India avoid going beyond 41 weeks to prevent complications related to placental aging.
