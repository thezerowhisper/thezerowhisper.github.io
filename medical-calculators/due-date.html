LMP vs Ultrasound vs IVF: Which Due Date Method is Most Accurate?

Indian obstetricians use up to four methods to calculate the Estimated Due Date (EDD). Accuracy varies significantly — knowing which method to trust helps avoid unnecessary interventions.

📌 FOGSI guideline (India): If the ultrasound EDD and LMP-based EDD differ by more than 7 days in the first trimester (or more than 14 days in the second trimester), the ultrasound EDD takes precedence. The EDD should not be changed more than once after this confirmation.

How to Calculate Your Due Date from LMP — Step by Step

  1. Note your LMP date: Record the first day of your Last Menstrual Period — this is the universal starting point used by all Indian obstetricians.
  2. Add 280 days (Naegele's Rule): For a standard 28-day cycle, EDD = LMP + 280 days. This equals adding 9 months and 7 days to the LMP date.
  3. Adjust for cycle length: If your cycle is 35 days (not 28), add 7 more days. Our calculator handles all cycle lengths from 21 to 45 days automatically.
  4. Confirm with dating scan: Get an ultrasound at 8–10 weeks. If the scan-based EDD differs by more than 7 days, your obstetrician will revise the EDD to match the scan.

🥗 Indian Pregnancy Nutrition Guide — Trimester by Trimester

First Trimester — Weeks 1 to 13

  • Folic acid 5 mg/day: Neural tube defect prevention — ideally started before conception and continued until 12 weeks. Available free at government hospitals
  • Iron + Folic Acid (IFA) tablet: ICMR recommends 180 mg elemental iron + 500 µg folic acid daily from first trimester to prevent anaemia, which affects 50%+ of Indian pregnant women
  • Nausea management: Ginger tea, jeera (cumin) water, and small frequent meals are safe. Avoid raw papaya, large amounts of pineapple, and sesame seeds which may stimulate uterine contractions

Second Trimester — Weeks 14 to 27

  • Calcium 1,000–1,200 mg/day: Essential for fetal bone and tooth formation. Sources: milk, curd, paneer, ragi (finger millet), til (sesame). Calcium carbonate supplements widely prescribed in India
  • Protein 70–100 g/day: Dal, rajma, chana, eggs, milk, paneer, soya. Most Indian women are protein-deficient in pregnancy — prioritise at every meal
  • GDM screening at 24–28 weeks: Indian women have 4× higher GDM risk. DIPSI 75g OGTT (non-fasting). If positive: low-glycaemic diet, walking 30 minutes daily, insulin if targets not met

Third Trimester — Weeks 28 to 40

  • Iron absorption tips: Take iron tablets at bedtime (not with milk or tea). Vitamin C (amla, lemon juice) doubles absorption. Dark stools are a normal side effect of iron tablets
  • Omega-3 (DHA): Walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds, fatty fish (safe 2×/week). Important for fetal brain and retinal development. Many obstetricians prescribe DHA 200–300 mg/day from second trimester
  • Absolute restrictions: Alcohol (zero safe amount), raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurised milk, excess Vitamin A (liver, cod liver oil), caffeine >200 mg/day (limit to under 2 cups of tea)

🚑 Signs of Labour — When to Go to Hospital

🚨 Go to hospital immediately for: heavy vaginal bleeding, severe headache with visual disturbance (possible pre-eclampsia), no fetal movement for >2 hours, cord prolapse, or severe abdominal pain at any gestation — do not wait for contractions.

True Labour Signs (after 37 weeks)

  • Regular contractions — the 5-1-1 rule: Contractions every 5 minutes, lasting 60 seconds each, for at least 1 hour. Starting in the back and radiating to the front, progressively stronger and longer
  • Rupture of membranes (PROM): Sudden gush or continuous trickle of clear/pale yellow fluid. Go to hospital within 1–2 hours even without contractions — risk of cord prolapse and infection increases after 18 hours
  • Show (bloody mucous plug): Passage of blood-stained mucus signals that the cervix is beginning to dilate. Labour usually starts within 24–48 hours. Not an emergency, but inform your obstetrician
  • Reduced fetal movements: Fewer than 10 movements in 2 hours at any point in the third trimester — go to hospital immediately for CTG (cardiotocography) monitoring

Kick Count — Self-Monitoring After 28 Weeks

Count fetal movements daily — aim for 10 movements within 2 hours after a meal, when the baby is typically most active. Keep a kick count diary in your MCP card. This is the most important self-monitoring tool in the third trimester and has been shown to detect fetal distress early.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions — Pregnancy Due Date Calculator

How is the due date calculated from LMP?
Using Naegele's Rule: add 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of the Last Menstrual Period (LMP), adjusted for cycle length. For a 28-day cycle this equals LMP + 9 months + 7 days. Our calculator automatically adjusts for cycle lengths of 21 to 45 days — longer cycles push the EDD later, shorter cycles bring it forward.
How accurate is ultrasound dating compared to LMP?
An early ultrasound measuring Crown-Rump Length (CRL) between 7 and 13 weeks is accurate to ±5–7 days and is the clinical gold standard for EDD. LMP dating is accurate to ±2 weeks and assumes a regular 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14. If the ultrasound EDD differs from the LMP EDD by more than 7 days, FOGSI and WHO guidelines recommend using the ultrasound-based date as the official EDD.
What is the due date formula for IVF Day 5 blastocyst transfer?
For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 261 days.
For a Day 3 cleavage embryo: EDD = Transfer Date + 263 days.
The general formula is: EDD = Transfer Date + (266 − embryo age at transfer). All pregnancies are 266 days from fertilisation to delivery. This method is the most accurate EDD calculation available.
When should I get my first antenatal check-up in India?
Ideally within 8–10 weeks of pregnancy. Your first ANC visit includes: blood tests (CBC, blood group and Rh type, random blood sugar, TSH, VDRL/RPR, HIV, Hepatitis B surface antigen), urine examination, blood pressure, weight, height, and a booking (dating) ultrasound scan. Register for your MCP (Mother and Child Protection) card and government schemes including PMMVY and Janani Suraksha Yojana at your nearest PHC, sub-centre, or Anganwadi.
What is the DIPSI protocol for gestational diabetes screening in India?
DIPSI (Diabetic Pregnancy Study Group India): Give 75g oral glucose at any time of day without requiring overnight fasting. Measure venous blood sugar at 2 hours. A result of ≥140 mg/dL = GDM. This method is preferred in India because Indian women have a GDM prevalence of 16–17% — approximately 4 times higher than Western populations — and the non-fasting test is more practical for field conditions.
Can my due date change after an early ultrasound?
Yes. If the first-trimester ultrasound EDD differs from the LMP-based EDD by more than 7 days, the EDD is revised to match the ultrasound date. If the difference appears for the first time in the second trimester (>14 days discrepancy), it is also revised. However, multiple revisions are not recommended — the first-trimester CRL scan is the most accurate single measurement and should be used as the definitive EDD throughout the pregnancy.
Is it safe to travel during pregnancy in India?
The safest period to travel is the second trimester (14–28 weeks) when nausea has passed and the risk of preterm labour is lower. Avoid long-distance travel after 36 weeks. Always carry your MCP card and obstetrician's emergency contact. On flights, walk every 2 hours to prevent DVT, wear compression stockings, and stay well-hydrated. Most Indian and international airlines permit travel up to 36 weeks with a medical fitness certificate from your obstetrician.
Which vaccines are recommended during pregnancy in India?
Tetanus toxoid (Td): 2 doses in the first pregnancy — first dose at 16–24 weeks, second dose 4 weeks later; or 1 booster dose if previously immunised. Influenza vaccine: Recommended in all trimesters. COVID-19 vaccine: Recommended per current NHP guidelines. All vaccines listed above are available free of charge at government antenatal clinics and sub-centres across India.
