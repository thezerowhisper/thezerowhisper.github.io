📐 What is Gestational Age? How to Calculate it from LMP, Ultrasound & CRL

Gestational age is the clinical measure of how far a pregnancy has progressed, always counted from the first day of the Last Menstrual Period (LMP) — not from conception. It is expressed in completed weeks plus days (e.g., 12 weeks 3 days, written as 12+3 or 12w3d). A full-term pregnancy is 40 weeks 0 days gestational age (280 days from LMP).

📌 Key fact: Gestational age always includes approximately 2 weeks before actual fertilisation. This is because ovulation typically occurs on Day 14 of a 28-day cycle, but gestational age is counted from Day 1 of the LMP. So at 4 weeks gestational age, the embryo is only about 2 weeks old (fetal/embryonic age).

How to Calculate Gestational Age from LMP

  1. Note Day 1 of your LMP — the universal starting point used by all obstetricians worldwide (ACOG, FOGSI, WHO)
  2. Count total days from LMP to today — for example, 77 days from LMP
  3. Divide by 7 — 77 ÷ 7 = 11 weeks 0 days. You are 11+0 gestational age
  4. Confirm with CRL ultrasound — if the CRL-based gestational age differs by more than 7 days, use the ultrasound value as the definitive gestational age

Gestational Age vs Fetal Age — What's the Difference?

Gestational age (GA) is always 2 weeks more than fetal age (also called embryonic age or conceptional age). Doctors use gestational age universally in scan reports, prescription notes, and hospital records. When a radiologist writes "GA 12+3 weeks" in a scan report, it means 12 weeks and 3 days from the LMP, not from fertilisation.

🥗 Indian Pregnancy Nutrition Guide — Trimester by Trimester

First Trimester — Gestational Age 1 to 13 Weeks

  • Folic acid 5 mg/day: Neural tube defect prevention — ideally started before conception and continued until 12 weeks gestational age. Available free at government hospitals
  • Iron + Folic Acid (IFA) tablet: ICMR recommends 180 mg elemental iron + 500 µg folic acid daily from first trimester to prevent anaemia, which affects 50%+ of Indian pregnant women
  • Nausea management: Ginger tea, jeera (cumin) water, and small frequent meals are safe. Avoid raw papaya, large amounts of pineapple, and sesame seeds which may stimulate uterine contractions

Second Trimester — Gestational Age 14 to 27 Weeks

  • Calcium 1,000–1,200 mg/day: Essential for fetal bone and tooth formation. Sources: milk, curd, paneer, ragi (finger millet), til (sesame). Calcium carbonate supplements widely prescribed in India
  • Protein 70–100 g/day: Dal, rajma, chana, eggs, milk, paneer, soya. Most Indian women are protein-deficient in pregnancy — prioritise at every meal
  • GDM screening at 24–28 weeks gestational age: Indian women have 4× higher GDM risk. DIPSI 75g OGTT (non-fasting). If positive: low-glycaemic diet, walking 30 minutes daily, insulin if targets not met

Third Trimester — Gestational Age 28 to 40 Weeks

  • Iron absorption tips: Take iron tablets at bedtime (not with milk or tea). Vitamin C (amla, lemon juice) doubles absorption. Dark stools are a normal side effect of iron tablets
  • Omega-3 (DHA): Walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds, fatty fish (safe 2×/week). Important for fetal brain and retinal development. Many obstetricians prescribe DHA 200–300 mg/day from second trimester
  • Absolute restrictions: Alcohol (zero safe amount), raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurised milk, excess Vitamin A (liver, cod liver oil), caffeine >200 mg/day (limit to under 2 cups of tea)

🚑 Signs of Labour — When to Go to Hospital

🚨 Go to hospital immediately for: heavy vaginal bleeding, severe headache with visual disturbance (possible pre-eclampsia), no fetal movement for >2 hours, cord prolapse, or severe abdominal pain at any gestational age — do not wait for contractions.

True Labour Signs (after 37 Weeks Gestational Age)

  • Regular contractions — the 5-1-1 rule: Contractions every 5 minutes, lasting 60 seconds each, for at least 1 hour. Starting in the back and radiating to the front, progressively stronger and longer
  • Rupture of membranes (PROM): Sudden gush or continuous trickle of clear/pale yellow fluid. Go to hospital within 1–2 hours even without contractions — risk of cord prolapse and infection increases after 18 hours
  • Show (bloody mucous plug): Passage of blood-stained mucus signals that the cervix is beginning to dilate. Labour usually starts within 24–48 hours. Not an emergency, but inform your obstetrician
  • Reduced fetal movements: Fewer than 10 movements in 2 hours at any point in the third trimester — go to hospital immediately for CTG (cardiotocography) monitoring

Kick Count — Self-Monitoring After 28 Weeks Gestational Age

Count fetal movements daily — aim for 10 movements within 2 hours after a meal, when the baby is typically most active. Keep a kick count diary in your MCP card. This is the most important self-monitoring tool in the third trimester and has been shown to detect fetal distress early.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions — Gestational Age & Due Date Calculator

What is gestational age and how is it calculated?
Gestational age is the standard clinical measure of how far a pregnancy has progressed, always counted from the first day of the Last Menstrual Period (LMP) — not from the date of conception. It is expressed as completed weeks plus days (e.g., 12 weeks 3 days, written as 12+3). To calculate gestational age: subtract the LMP date from today's date to get total days, then divide by 7. At 40 weeks 0 days gestational age, a pregnancy is at its Estimated Due Date (EDD).
How many weeks pregnant am I? How to calculate gestational age today
To find out how many weeks pregnant you are today: (1) Note Day 1 of your Last Menstrual Period. (2) Count total days from that date to today. (3) Divide by 7 — the whole number is your completed weeks, and the remainder is additional days. Example: 77 days from LMP = 11 weeks 0 days gestational age (11+0). Use the LMP tab above to calculate instantly.
How do I calculate gestational age from CRL on ultrasound?
Gestational age from CRL (Crown-Rump Length) uses the Hadlock formula: GA (weeks) = 5.2876 + (0.1584 × CRL in mm) − (0.0007 × CRL²). This applies from approximately 6 to 13+6 weeks (CRL 10–84 mm). It is accurate to ±3–5 days and is the gold standard for pregnancy dating per ISUOG, ACOG, and FOGSI. Use the "CRL / BPD" tab in this calculator to enter your CRL measurement in mm and get gestational age instantly.
What is the difference between gestational age and fetal age?
Gestational age is counted from Day 1 of the LMP and is approximately 2 weeks more than fetal age. Fetal age (also called embryonic age or conceptional age) counts from fertilisation. At 12 weeks gestational age, fetal age is approximately 10 weeks. Doctors, radiologists, and scan reports always use gestational age — when a report says "GA 20+3," it means 20 weeks and 3 days from LMP.
How is the due date calculated from LMP?
Using Naegele's Rule: add 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of the Last Menstrual Period (LMP), adjusted for cycle length. For a 28-day cycle this equals LMP + 9 months + 7 days. Our calculator automatically adjusts for cycle lengths of 21 to 45 days — longer cycles push the EDD later, shorter cycles bring it forward.
How accurate is ultrasound dating compared to LMP?
An early ultrasound measuring Crown-Rump Length (CRL) between 7 and 13 weeks is accurate to ±3–5 days and is the clinical gold standard for gestational age and EDD. LMP dating is accurate to ±2 weeks and assumes a regular 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14. If the ultrasound EDD differs from the LMP EDD by more than 7 days, FOGSI, ACOG, and WHO guidelines all recommend using the ultrasound-based date as the official EDD.
What gestational age is considered full term, preterm, and post-term?
Per ACOG and FOGSI definitions: Preterm = <37+0 weeks gestational age; Early term = 37+0 to 38+6 weeks; Full term = 39+0 to 40+6 weeks; Late term = 41+0 to 41+6 weeks; Post-term = ≥42+0 weeks. Most Indian centres offer induction of labour at 41+0 weeks gestational age to reduce post-dates complications.
What is the due date formula for IVF Day 5 blastocyst transfer?
For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 261 days.
For a Day 3 cleavage embryo: EDD = Transfer Date + 263 days.
General formula: EDD = Transfer Date + (266 − embryo age at transfer). Gestational age for IVF pregnancies is calculated as: days since transfer + embryo age + 14 days (LMP equivalent), expressed as weeks + days.
When should I get my first antenatal check-up in India?
Ideally within 8–10 weeks gestational age. Your first ANC visit includes: blood tests (CBC, blood group and Rh type, random blood sugar, TSH, VDRL/RPR, HIV, Hepatitis B surface antigen), urine examination, blood pressure, weight, height, and a booking (dating) ultrasound scan. Register for your MCP (Mother and Child Protection) card and government schemes including PMMVY and Janani Suraksha Yojana at your nearest PHC, sub-centre, or Anganwadi.
What is the DIPSI protocol for gestational diabetes screening in India?
DIPSI (Diabetic Pregnancy Study Group India): Give 75g oral glucose at any time of day without requiring overnight fasting. Measure venous blood sugar at 2 hours. A result of ≥140 mg/dL = GDM. This is performed at 24–28 weeks gestational age. The method is preferred in India because Indian women have a GDM prevalence of 16–17% — approximately 4 times higher than Western populations — and the non-fasting test is more practical for field conditions.
Can my due date change after an early ultrasound?
Yes. If the first-trimester ultrasound EDD differs from the LMP-based EDD by more than 7 days, the EDD is revised to match the ultrasound date. If the difference first appears in the second trimester (>14 days discrepancy), it is also revised. However, multiple revisions are not recommended — the first-trimester CRL scan provides the most accurate gestational age and should be used as the definitive EDD throughout the pregnancy.
Is it safe to travel during pregnancy in India?
The safest period to travel is the second trimester (14–28 weeks gestational age) when nausea has passed and the risk of preterm labour is lower. Avoid long-distance travel after 36 weeks. Always carry your MCP card and obstetrician's emergency contact. On flights, walk every 2 hours to prevent DVT, wear compression stockings, and stay well-hydrated. Most airlines permit travel up to 36 weeks gestational age with a medical fitness certificate.
Which vaccines are recommended during pregnancy in India?
Tetanus toxoid (Td): 2 doses in the first pregnancy — first dose at 16–24 weeks gestational age, second dose 4 weeks later; or 1 booster dose if previously immunised. Influenza vaccine: Recommended in all trimesters. COVID-19 vaccine: Recommended per current NHP guidelines. All vaccines listed above are available free of charge at government antenatal clinics and sub-centres across India.
