What is the Mentzer Index?

The Mentzer Index is a widely used hematological calculation to differentiate between two common causes of microcytic anemia: Beta Thalassemia Trait and Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA). Both conditions present with low MCV (microcytosis), making them difficult to distinguish on a standard CBC without further testing.

The Formula

Mentzer Index = MCV (fL) ÷ RBC Count (×10⁶/μL)

Interpretation Guide

Index Value Likely Diagnosis Clinical Rationale
< 13 Beta Thalassemia Trait The bone marrow produces many small, fragile red blood cells. (RBC is high, MCV is low).
13 - 15 Borderline / Indeterminate Cannot reliably distinguish. Further testing (Ferritin, Hb Electrophoresis) is required.
> 15 Iron Deficiency Anemia The bone marrow cannot produce enough cells due to lack of iron. (Both RBC and MCV are low).

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the Mentzer Index?

The Mentzer index has a sensitivity of approximately 90-95% and specificity of 85-90%. It is an excellent screening tool, but definitive diagnosis requires iron studies and Hemoglobin electrophoresis (or HPLC).

Why is it important to differentiate the two?

Giving iron supplements to a patient with Thalassemia Trait is not only ineffective but can lead to dangerous iron overload over time. Conversely, IDA requires prompt iron replacement.
