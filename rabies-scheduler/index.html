What to Do Immediately After a Dog Bite or Scratch

1. Wash the wound immediately with soap and running water for at least 15 minutes. This is critical.

2. Visit a doctor immediately — do not wait or observe the animal first.

3. Start ARV on Day 0 — the same day as the bite if possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rabies injections are needed in India?

4 anti-rabies vaccine (ARV) injections are required for a healthy person — given on Days 0, 3, 7, and 28 after the first dose. This is the intramuscular (IM) Essen regimen used across India.

What is the dog bite injection schedule in India?

The anti rabies timeline after a dog bite in India is: Day 0 (immediately), Day 3, Day 7, and Day 28. Use this calculator to get your exact ARV injection dates automatically mapped to a calendar.

What is the difference between Intradermal vs Intramuscular schedule?

The Intramuscular (IM) schedule used in this calculator is 4 visits (Days 0, 3, 7, 28). Some government hospitals use the Intradermal (ID) schedule which requires only 3 visits (Days 0, 3, 7), but injects small amounts at two different sites on the body each time.

What if I miss a rabies vaccine dose date?

Take the missed ARV dose as soon as possible and continue the remaining doses on the original scheduled intervals. Do not restart the series. Consult your doctor if you are severely delayed.

Is rabies 100% fatal without vaccination?

Yes — once symptoms of rabies appear, it is almost always fatal. Timely ARV post-exposure prophylaxis is fully effective in preventing the disease if started promptly after exposure.

Do I need rabies immunoglobulin (RIG) along with the ARV?

RIG is required for Category III exposures (deep bites, puncture wounds, licks on mucous membranes or broken skin). It is given only once on Day 0, alongside the first ARV dose, to provide immediate antibodies while the vaccine takes effect.

What are rabies prevention shots after a dog bite?

Rabies prevention shots refer to the anti-rabies vaccine (ARV) given after exposure to prevent rabies infection. In India, this includes 4 doses on Days 0, 3, 7, and 28 as part of post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP).
