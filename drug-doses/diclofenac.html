Diclofenac — Clinical Guide India

Diclofenac sodium is the most widely prescribed NSAID in India, used across surgery, orthopaedics, gynaecology, urology, dentistry, and general practice. It is available as Voveran, Diclogesic, and Dynapar in oral, IM injection, topical, and suppository forms. It inhibits both COX-1 and COX-2, providing anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects. While effective, diclofenac carries the same class-specific risks as all NSAIDs — gastrointestinal bleeding, nephrotoxicity, and cardiovascular risk — and is one of the most commonly implicated drugs in NSAID-related acute kidney injury and peptic ulcer bleeding in Indian hospitals.

IM injection — the most important safety rule

Diclofenac injection (Voveran 75mg/3ml) is an intramuscular (IM) preparation only. It must never be given intravenously. IV diclofenac causes severe hypotension and anaphylaxis and has caused deaths. Despite this, IV administration errors continue to occur in India, particularly in settings where the injection is given by non-medical staff. The injection must be given deep into the upper outer quadrant of the gluteal muscle (not the thigh). IM diclofenac should not be used for more than 2 consecutive days — switch to oral diclofenac thereafter to reduce injection-site complications (haematoma, abscess, sciatic nerve injury).

Renal colic — diclofenac as first-line IM analgesic

Diclofenac 75mg IM is the first-line analgesic for renal colic in adults in Indian emergency departments, preferred over opioids in patients without contraindications. NSAIDs reduce renal pelvic pressure and ureteric spasm by inhibiting prostaglandin-mediated smooth muscle contraction. A single IM dose provides excellent pain relief within 20–30 minutes and may be repeated once after 4–6 hours. Ensure the patient is not dehydrated and has no pre-existing renal impairment before use.

Topical diclofenac — underutilised in India

Topical diclofenac 1% gel (Voveran Emugel, Dynapar gel) is highly effective for localised musculoskeletal pain (osteoarthritis of the knee, soft tissue injuries, back pain) and has significantly lower systemic absorption than oral diclofenac, reducing GI and renal risks. A Cochrane review found topical diclofenac equivalent to oral NSAIDs for knee OA pain relief. For elderly patients, those with CKD, or those with GI risk — topical diclofenac is a safer first-line option than oral. Apply 2–4g (approximately 1–2 cm ribbon of gel) to the affected area 3–4 times daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the diclofenac injection dose and how is it given?+
Diclofenac injection: 75 mg (one standard ampoule, 3 ml) given deep IM into the upper outer quadrant of the gluteal muscle. Use a long enough needle (21G, 3.8cm or longer) to ensure deep gluteal injection. Never give into the thigh (risk of sciatic nerve injury). Never give IV. Maximum 2 injections per day (150mg total). Switch to oral diclofenac after 1–2 days of IM treatment.
Should PPI cover be prescribed with diclofenac?+
Yes — patients at increased GI risk should receive PPI co-prescription with diclofenac. High-risk patients requiring PPI cover: age over 60, history of peptic ulcer, concurrent aspirin or corticosteroid, or any two moderate-risk factors. Use pantoprazole 40mg once daily or omeprazole 20mg once daily. For short courses (1–3 days) in low-risk adults, PPI cover is not mandatory but should be considered.
Can diclofenac be used in children?+
Diclofenac is not routinely recommended in children under 14 years in India. Ibuprofen and paracetamol are the preferred analgesics in children. For adolescents aged 14+ years, adult dosing can be used. Diclofenac gel (topical) may be used under specialist advice in older children. Avoid in children with chickenpox, dengue, or CKD for the same reasons as other NSAIDs.
What is the difference between diclofenac and diclofenac SR tablets?+
Diclofenac 50mg IR (immediate-release) reaches peak blood levels faster and is suitable for acute pain requiring rapid onset. It must be given 2–3 times daily due to its short half-life. Diclofenac SR (sustained-release) 75mg or 100mg releases the drug slowly, requiring only once or twice daily dosing — better for chronic pain and improved compliance. SR tablets should not be crushed or chewed. For acute severe pain (renal colic, post-op), IR or IM forms are preferred. For arthritis maintenance, SR is preferred.
⚠️Diclofenac IM injection is NEVER to be given IV. Avoid in CKD, dengue, GI ulcer, cardiac failure, and third trimester pregnancy. Use lowest effective dose for shortest duration. Verify against BNF and current prescribing information.
