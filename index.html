Why These Calculators Use Indian & International Guidelines

Many standard calculators use Western or WHO cutoffs not always appropriate for all clinical settings.

⚖️
Indian BMI Cutoffs
Indians develop metabolic disease at lower BMI values. Overweight ≥23, obese ≥25 — not the global 25/30 thresholds.
💉
NCDC Rabies Protocol
Rabies PEP follows the 4-dose Essen regimen per NCDC India and WHO 2018, covering category I, II, and III wounds.
🤰
FOGSI / DIPSI Obstetrics
Obstetric tools follow FOGSI and DIPSI protocols used in Indian clinical practice, alongside ACOG references.
🌍
Internationally Validated Scores
CURB-65, NEWS2, GCS, Wells Score follow BTS, RCP, and NICE guidelines — validated and widely used in Indian tertiary care.
