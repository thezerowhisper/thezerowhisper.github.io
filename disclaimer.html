⚕️ For Healthcare Professionals Only

All clinical calculators, scoring tools, drug dose references, and decision-support content on RxMedCalc (rxmedcalc.com) are intended for use by qualified healthcare professionals — including registered medical practitioners, nurses, pharmacists, and allied health professionals — as clinical decision-support aids only.

These tools are not intended for self-diagnosis, self-treatment, or use by the general public without the guidance of a licensed healthcare provider. If you are a patient, please consult your doctor or pharmacist before acting on any information from this website.

🧮 No Substitute for Clinical Judgement

Every clinical calculator on this site produces an output based on the values entered. These outputs are reference values only — they do not account for the full clinical picture, patient history, physical examination findings, comorbidities, local drug availability, or institution-specific protocols.

Clinical decisions — including drug dosing, admission decisions, referral thresholds, and treatment plans — must always be made by a qualified clinician who has examined the patient and is familiar with the relevant guidelines applicable in their practice setting.

Never act solely on the output of a calculator without independent clinical verification.

📋 Accuracy & Guideline Adherence

Every tool on RxMedCalc is built against referenced clinical guidelines, including those from NCDC India, IAP, FOGSI, WHO, NICE, BTS, RCP, CKD-EPI, ADA, RSSDI, AKIN/KDIGO, and other national and international bodies. The specific guideline reference for each calculator is noted on its page.

Despite our best efforts to maintain accuracy, clinical guidelines are updated periodically. There may be a lag between a guideline update and its reflection on this website. Always cross-reference with the most current version of the relevant guideline before clinical application.

If you identify an error, an outdated value, or a discrepancy with current guidelines, please report it via the Contact page. We take clinical accuracy seriously and will investigate and correct errors promptly.

💊 Drug Dosing Caution

Drug dose calculators on this site provide weight-based or age-based reference doses based on standard formularies and published guidelines. However:

  • Doses may need adjustment for renal impairment, hepatic impairment, extremes of age, pregnancy, and drug interactions.
  • Drug availability and approved indications vary by country and institution.
  • Always verify doses against the current drug package insert, local formulary, or a clinical pharmacist before prescribing or administering.
  • Chemotherapy and other high-alert medications require independent verification by an oncologist and clinical pharmacist before administration. Never administer chemotherapy based solely on a calculator result.

🌍 Jurisdiction & Applicability

Many tools on RxMedCalc are built with Indian clinical practice in mind — using NCDC, IAP, FOGSI, and Jindal spirometry reference values where applicable. However, the site is accessed globally. Clinical cutoffs, drug schedules, and management protocols differ across countries. It is the clinician's responsibility to verify that the values and protocols used are appropriate for their jurisdiction and patient population.

For example: rabies PEP schedules follow NCDC India (4-dose Essen IM regimen), which may differ from WHO or local health authority protocols in other countries. BMI cutoffs use Indian population thresholds, which differ from WHO global cutoffs.
