Biotin (Vitamin B7) — Clinical Guide for India

Biotin is a water-soluble B-vitamin that serves as a coenzyme for five critical carboxylase enzymes involved in gluconeogenesis, fatty acid synthesis, and amino acid catabolism. Isolated biotin deficiency is rare in healthy individuals eating a varied diet, but biotinidase deficiency — a treatable inborn error of metabolism detectable on newborn screening — requires prompt lifelong treatment to prevent irreversible neurological damage.

True biotin deficiency — when does it occur?

True biotin deficiency is uncommon but occurs in: prolonged consumption of raw egg whites (avidin in raw egg whites binds and blocks biotin absorption), prolonged parenteral nutrition without biotin supplementation, biotinidase deficiency (genetic), prolonged anticonvulsant therapy (valproate and carbamazepine impair biotin metabolism), and alcoholism with malabsorption. Clinical features include: seborrhoeic dermatitis, alopecia, conjunctivitis, ataxia, hypotonia, and in severe cases seizures and developmental regression.

Biotinidase deficiency — a treatable newborn emergency

Biotinidase deficiency is an autosomal recessive disorder in which biotin cannot be recycled from carboxylase enzymes, leading to functional deficiency despite normal dietary intake. Profound deficiency (enzyme activity <10% of normal) presents with seizures, hypotonia, alopecia, skin rash, hearing loss, and optic atrophy if untreated. Treatment with oral free biotin 5–20 mg/day is highly effective when started early — ideally detected on newborn screening. Partial deficiency (10–30% activity) is treated with lower doses and may have a milder course.

High-dose biotin for hair and nails — separating evidence from marketing

Biotin is extensively marketed in India for hair fall and brittle nails at doses of 5000–10,000 mcg (5–10 mg/day). The evidence base is limited to case reports and small uncontrolled studies in patients with documented biotin deficiency — most of whom had underlying causes like biotinidase deficiency or raw egg consumption. There is no robust evidence that high-dose biotin supplementation benefits hair growth or nail quality in individuals with normal biotin levels. Importantly, high-dose biotin causes false laboratory results in many commonly ordered tests (see sidebar), a risk that is widely underappreciated in Indian clinical practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does biotin interfere with blood tests?+
Many laboratory immunoassays use biotin-streptavidin binding technology as part of the detection method. When excess biotin is present in the blood from high-dose supplements (≥5 mg), it competes with the test reagents, producing falsely high or falsely low results. This affects thyroid tests (TSH, T4), cardiac markers (troponin), reproductive hormones (LH, FSH, prolactin, HCG), vitamin D, ferritin, and PTH. The FDA issued a safety communication in 2017. Stop biotin for at least 2 days (preferably 7 days for high doses) before any blood test, and inform the lab.
Is biotin safe during pregnancy?+
Biotin at the recommended dietary intake (35 mcg/day in pregnancy) is safe. High-dose supplementation (5–10 mg/day) in pregnancy has not been adequately studied for safety. Animal studies suggest high-dose biotin may interfere with fatty acid metabolism during embryogenesis, though clinical significance in humans is unclear. Given the lack of evidence for benefit in non-deficient patients and theoretical concerns, high-dose biotin supplementation is not recommended in pregnancy unless there is documented deficiency or biotinidase deficiency.
How is biotinidase deficiency diagnosed and treated in India?+
Biotinidase deficiency is detected on newborn screening programmes (now available in several Indian states). Confirmatory testing: serum biotinidase enzyme activity assay, followed by genetic testing (BTD gene). Profound deficiency (<10% activity): free biotin 5–20 mg/day immediately — do not delay pending genetics. Partial deficiency (10–30%): biotin 1–5 mg/day. Treatment is lifelong and highly effective when started early. Untreated profound deficiency causes irreversible neurological damage within months. Key centres in India: AIIMS Delhi Biochemical Genetics, CMC Vellore, NIMHANS.
⚠️High-dose biotin (≥5 mg/day) causes false laboratory results — stop 2–7 days before blood tests. True biotin deficiency is rare; most hair-loss indications lack evidence. Biotinidase deficiency requires specialist IEM management.
